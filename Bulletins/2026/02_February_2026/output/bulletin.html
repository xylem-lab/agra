Bulletin Overview

Overview

This report summarizes yield, acreage, and total production estimates for Maize, Rice, Beans across focus countries. The data is updated monthly in the RFBS Dashboard via a standard data file. Our model reports on the main growing season for countries with available historical yield data for model training.

Data Limitations and Model Caveats

The data used in this report has limitations due to gaps in historical records and variations in data collection methods:

  • Historical data accuracy and consistency vary by country
  • Our model reflects these data gaps, as it is trained on available information
  • We view these limitations as opportunities to improve data sharing and scaling methods with our country partners

Current Focus: Data presented this month covers Maize, Rice, Beans systems in United Republic Of Tanzania, Kenya, Zambia, Rwanda, Malawi, which are currently in season.

Note on excluded countries: Uganda is not included in this month's bulletin. This country is currently post-harvest or out of the growing season — once harvesting begins, vegetation indices (NDVI/EVI) are no longer reliable predictors of crop yield and model forecasts are suspended until the next season commences.

Tanzania — Vuli season not covered: This bulletin reports Tanzania's Msimu season only. The Vuli season is currently out of season for February — crops are either pre-planting or post-harvest and satellite-based yield forecasts are not applicable. The Vuli season will be covered in a future bulletin once it becomes active.

Summary

Executive Summary

Current projections for February 2026 Beans, Maize, Rice production in Kenya, Malawi, Rwanda, United Republic Of Tanzania, and Zambia show varying forecasts between USDA, FAO, and UMD-XylemLab models, as summarized in the Table and Figure below. For maize, UMD-XylemLab generally projects higher yields and wider production ranges in Malawi (2.75-4.71 million MT) and Zambia (3.0-4.47 million MT) compared to USDA and FAO, which remain closely aligned. Kenya's maize estimates show UMD-XylemLab indicating a broader production range (3.53-6.42 million MT) with a slightly lower yield than USDA and FAO. Rwanda's maize projections by UMD show lower totals (0.3-0.68 million MT) relative to USDA and FAO. Rice production forecasts diverge notably, with UMD-XylemLab presenting significantly lower estimates in Kenya (0.02-0.05 million MT) than USDA and FAO (around 4 million MT), likely reflecting sensitivity to different agro-ecological zones. Malawi and Rwanda show more comparable rice estimates across FAO and UMD, though UMD's ranges remain wider. Beans are solely reported from UMD-XylemLab, revealing stable yet modest production levels across all countries, ranging from 0.04-0.16 million MT in Tanzania to 0.65-1.58 million MT in Kenya. Differences in model geographic scope and seasonal transitions account for variability in forecasts. Overall, the data illustrates both regional stability and model-driven discrepancies, emphasizing the need for integrated approaches to capture diverse production conditions.

Beans cover smaller areas, with Central (181,339 ha), Southern (119,093 ha), and Northern (67,015 ha); mean predicted productions peak in Central (94,296 tons), Southern (109,566 tons), and Northern (46,240 tons). Rice has the smallest cultivation area with Central (39,510 ha), Southern (25,948 ha), and Northern (14,601 ha); mean productions range from 25,948 tons in Southern to 58,475 tons in Central. Rice shows the highest variability given wide standard errors and significant negative anomalies, indicating increased uncertainty in yields, whereas maize and beans maintain stable trends across regions.
