Bulletin Overview

Overview

This report summarizes yield, acreage, and total production estimates for Maize, Beans, Rice across focus countries. The data is updated monthly in the RFBS Dashboard via a standard data file. Our model reports on the main growing season for countries with available historical yield data for model training.

Data Limitations and Model Caveats

The data used in this report has limitations due to gaps in historical records and variations in data collection methods:

  • Historical data accuracy and consistency vary by country
  • Our model reflects these data gaps, as it is trained on available information
  • We view these limitations as opportunities to improve data sharing and scaling methods with our country partners

Current Focus: Data presented this month covers Maize, Beans, Rice systems in Zambia, Rwanda, United Republic Of Tanzania, Malawi, Kenya, which are currently in season.

Note on excluded countries: Uganda is not included in this month's bulletin. This country is currently post-harvest or out of the growing season — once harvesting begins, vegetation indices (NDVI/EVI) are no longer reliable predictors of crop yield and model forecasts are suspended until the next season commences.

Tanzania — Vuli season not covered: This bulletin reports Tanzania's Msimu season only. The Vuli season is currently out of season for February — crops are either pre-planting or post-harvest and satellite-based yield forecasts are not applicable. The Vuli season will be covered in a future bulletin once it becomes active.

Summary

Executive Summary

Current projections for February 2026 Beans, Maize, Rice production in Kenya, Malawi, Rwanda, United Republic Of Tanzania, Zambia show varying forecasts between USDA, FAO and UMD-XylemLab models, as summarized in Table and Figure below. For maize, UMD-XylemLab estimates suggest larger ranges in Malawi (2.41–5.39 million MT) and Zambia (2.43–5.22 million MT) compared to USDA (3.0 and 3.4 million MT) and FAO (3.5 and 3.3 million MT), reflecting greater uncertainty or broader regional inclusion. Kenya's maize forecast by UMD-XylemLab is notably wider (2.74–6.82 million MT) than USDA (4.4 million MT) and FAO (4.28 million MT) figures, while Rwanda's maize estimates are relatively close across sources but with UMD-XylemLab indicating a lower upper range (0.31–0.69 million MT). Rice projections show consistent FAO and USDA estimates in Kenya and Rwanda, but UMD-XylemLab yields lower ranges, particularly in Kenya (0.01–0.05 million MT versus about 4 million MT USDA/FAO), likely due to differences in geographic or crop type focus. Beans are principally reported by UMD-XylemLab, displaying moderate production with ranges from 0.05–0.18 million MT in Tanzania to 0.42–1.81 million MT in Kenya, highlighting stable but localized variability. Overall, these forecasts underscore divergent modeling approaches and regional considerations, with UMD-XylemLab often reflecting broader uncertainty and geographic specificity compared to USDA and FAO single-value estimates.

The stable trends across crops suggest consistency in production forecasts, yet negative anomalies in maize and rice in specific counties highlight areas needing close monitoring for possible stress impacts.
