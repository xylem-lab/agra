Bulletin Overview

Overview

This report summarizes yield, acreage, and total production estimates for Rice, Beans, Maize across focus countries. The data is updated monthly in the RFBS Dashboard via a standard data file. Our model reports on the main growing season for countries with available historical yield data for model training.

Data Limitations and Model Caveats

The data used in this report has limitations due to gaps in historical records and variations in data collection methods:

  • Historical data accuracy and consistency vary by country
  • Our model reflects these data gaps, as it is trained on available information
  • We view these limitations as opportunities to improve data sharing and scaling methods with our country partners

Current Focus: Data presented this month covers Rice, Beans, Maize systems in Rwanda, Kenya, Uganda, Malawi, United Republic Of Tanzania, which are currently in season.

Note on excluded countries: Zambia is not included in this month's bulletin. This country is currently post-harvest or out of the growing season — once harvesting begins, vegetation indices (NDVI/EVI) are no longer reliable predictors of crop yield and model forecasts are suspended until the next season commences.

Summary

Executive Summary

Current projections for April 2026 Beans, Maize, Rice production in Kenya, Malawi, Rwanda, Uganda, United Republic Of Tanzania show varying forecasts between USDA, FAO and UMD-XylemLab models, as summarized in Table and Figure below. For maize, USDA and FAO estimate Malawi's production around 3.0 to 3.5 million MT, whereas UMD shows a wider range of 2.82 to 5.07 million MT driven by a slightly larger area and higher yields. Kenya's maize production estimates vary, with USDA and FAO close at about 4.3 to 4.4 million MT, while UMD's broader range (3.53 to 6.59 million MT) reflects differences in area assumptions. Uganda presents USDA maize at 5.0 million MT, FAO much lower at 2.8 million MT due to smaller area, and UMD between 3.15 and 4.23 million MT. Rwanda’s maize shows stability around 0.45 to 0.51 million MT by USDA/FAO and a wider UMD range down to 0.22 million MT. Tanzania’s maize estimates from UMD span 3.61 to 8.55 million MT, reflecting the model's geographic scope. Rice estimates reveal FAO reporting slightly higher yields in Malawi and Kenya compared to UMD, with Malawi rice ranging 0.12 to 0.26 million MT and Kenya showing a stark difference—FAO at about 4.05 million MT and UMD substantially lower (0.02 to 0.11 million MT), likely due to coastal versus highland area sensitivities. Uganda's rice production estimates vary considerably, from 2.75 million MT (FAO) to a lower UMD range of 0.34 to 0.54 million MT. Beans estimates, available from UMD only, vary widely with Kenya ranging from 0.09 to 5.82 million MT, Uganda 0.66 to 2.56 million MT, and Rwanda lower between 0.16 and 0.9 million MT, reflecting differences in area and yield inputs. Overall, these projections highlight the influence of geographic scope, variable area, and yield assumptions between USDA, FAO, and UMD-XylemLab models, underscoring ongoing disparities in crop production forecasts across East African countries for April 2026.
