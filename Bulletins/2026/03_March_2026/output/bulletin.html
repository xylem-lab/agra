Bulletin Overview

Overview

This report summarizes yield, acreage, and total production estimates for Maize, Rice, Beans across focus countries. The data is updated monthly in the RFBS Dashboard via a standard data file. Our model reports on the main growing season for countries with available historical yield data for model training.

Data Limitations and Model Caveats

The data used in this report has limitations due to gaps in historical records and variations in data collection methods:

  • Historical data accuracy and consistency vary by country
  • Our model reflects these data gaps, as it is trained on available information
  • We view these limitations as opportunities to improve data sharing and scaling methods with our country partners

Current Focus: Data presented this month covers Maize, Rice, Beans systems in Rwanda, United Republic Of Tanzania, Zambia, Kenya, Malawi, Uganda, which are currently in season.

Summary

Executive Summary

Current projections for March 2026 Beans, Maize, Rice production in Kenya, Malawi, Rwanda, Uganda, United Republic Of Tanzania, Zambia show varying forecasts between USDA, FAO and UMD-XylemLab models, as summarized in Table and Figure below. For maize, USDA and FAO estimates are generally consistent in Malawi and Zambia, with Malawi’s production around 3.0 to 3.5 million MT and Zambia near 3.3 to 3.4 million MT, while UMD-XylemLab projects wider ranges reflecting higher uncertainty, notably Malawi from 2.42 to 5.38 million MT and Zambia from 2.43 to 5.21 million MT. Kenya and Uganda maize production show divergence as well; USDA and FAO report 4.3 to 5.0 million MT, whereas UMD-XylemLab presents a broader spread (Kenya: 2.88 to 6.74 million MT; Uganda: 2.76 to 5.68 million MT), influenced by geographic scope and seasonal shifts. Rice forecasts show FAO estimates higher than UMD-XylemLab in Kenya and Uganda, for example Kenya’s rice production is 4.05 to 5.8 million MT by FAO/USDA, but only 0.01 to 0.05 million MT by UMD, likely due to differing area coverage. Beans production is reported solely by UMD-XylemLab, revealing variability with Kenya and Uganda displaying higher production ranges (Kenya: 0.42 to 1.8 million MT; Uganda: 1.08 to 4.31 million MT) compared to Malawi and Tanzania with smaller ranges. Tanzania’s UMD-XylemLab maize estimates (1.7 to 3.74 million MT) are comparatively lower, reflecting a narrower geographic focus. Overall, the March 2026 projections underscore differences driven by model scope, data inputs, and regional production dynamics across crops and countries.

Model Performance Summary

Our yield prediction model is evaluated using historical data to assess accuracy and reliability. Performance metrics are calculated by comparing predicted yields against actual reported yields from previous seasons. The ensemble approach combines multiple remote sensing inputs (NDVI, ESI, precipitation, soil moisture) across a random forest architecture trained per country and crop.

Metrics Explanation

  • R² Score: Coefficient of determination (0–1); values above 0.7 indicate strong predictive power.
  • RMSE: Root Mean Square Error — average magnitude of prediction errors in MT/ha. Lower is better.
  • MAE: Mean Absolute Error — average absolute difference between predicted and actual yields in MT/ha. Lower is better.
